# How Long Does a Kitchen Remodel Take? Realistic 2026 Timelines

How long does a kitchen remodel take? It is the first question nearly every homeowner asks, and for good reason. Your kitchen is the most-used room in the house, and living without it for weeks or months affects your entire daily routine. The honest answer is that most kitchen remodels take between 6 and 12 weeks from demolition to final walkthrough, but the actual timeline depends heavily on the scope of work, material lead times, and permit requirements in your area. We have managed hundreds of kitchen renovations across San Diego, and we have learned that setting realistic expectations from day one is the single most important factor in a stress-free remodel.

## Timeline by Project Scope

The timeline varies dramatically based on what you are actually changing. A cabinet refresh — where you keep the existing layout and replace or reface cabinets, swap hardware, and update countertops — typically takes 2 to 3 weeks. A mid-range remodel that includes new cabinets, countertops, backsplash, flooring, and updated appliances while keeping the same footprint runs 6 to 8 weeks. A full gut renovation where everything is stripped to the studs, including new plumbing and electrical, takes 10 to 14 weeks. And if you are changing the layout — moving walls, relocating plumbing, or expanding into adjacent rooms — expect 12 to 16 weeks minimum. Understanding which category your project falls into is the first step toward building an accurate schedule.

## Phase-by-Phase Breakdown

Breaking the kitchen remodel timeline into phases makes the process easier to understand and track:

- **Design & Planning**: 2–4 weeks
- **Permits**: 2–6 weeks
- **Demolition**: 2–3 days
- **Rough-In (plumbing, electrical, gas)**: 1–2 weeks
- **Drywall & Paint**: 1 week
- **Cabinet Installation**: 1–2 weeks
- **Countertop Fabrication & Install**: 1 week (after 2–3 week fabrication lead time)
- **Fixtures & Appliances**: 3–5 days
- **Final Touches**: 3–5 days

Each phase builds on the previous one, which is why sequencing matters as much as speed.

## What Causes Delays

Understanding what causes delays helps you avoid them. Permit backlogs are one of the most common culprits — in busy seasons, San Diego jurisdictions can take 4 to 6 weeks for plan review compared to the typical 2 to 3 weeks. Material lead times are another major factor: stock cabinets ship in 1 to 2 weeks, semi-custom cabinets take 4 to 6 weeks, and fully custom cabinets can take 8 to 12 weeks from order to delivery. Natural stone countertops require 2 to 3 weeks for fabrication after templates are made. Discoveries behind walls — outdated wiring, corroded plumbing, water damage, or inadequate framing — can add days or weeks to the rough-in phase. And change orders, where homeowners alter decisions mid-project, are the most preventable cause of delays.

## How to Speed Up Your Kitchen Remodel

There are several proven strategies to keep your kitchen renovation timeline on track. First, finalize every selection before demolition begins — cabinets, countertops, tile, fixtures, appliances, paint colors, hardware, and lighting should all be chosen and ordered before a single wall comes down. Second, order long-lead materials as early as possible; if custom cabinets take 8 weeks, place that order during the permit phase so they arrive when rough-in is complete. Third, resist the temptation to make changes once construction starts. Even a seemingly small switch, like changing cabinet door style, can cascade into weeks of delay. Fourth, maintain a contingency of 10 to 15 percent in both your budget and timeline to absorb unexpected discoveries without derailing the project.

## Can You Live at Home During a Remodel?

Many homeowners ask whether they can continue living at home during a kitchen remodel, and the answer is almost always yes with some preparation. Set up a temporary kitchen in another room — a folding table, microwave, toaster oven, electric kettle, and a utility sink or bathroom provide the basics. Stock up on paper plates and disposable utensils to minimize cleanup without a functioning kitchen sink. Seal off the construction zone with plastic sheeting and zipper doors to contain dust, which is especially important during demolition and drywall phases. Plan to eat out or order delivery more frequently, and budget an extra $500 to $1,000 for meals during the project. The inconvenience is real but manageable, and most of our clients find it far preferable to the cost and hassle of temporary housing.

## San Diego Permit Timeline Specifics

San Diego permit timelines deserve special attention because they vary significantly by jurisdiction. The City of San Diego typically processes residential kitchen remodel permits in 2 to 4 weeks for over-the-counter submissions and 4 to 8 weeks for plan check. Escondido, Carlsbad, Encinitas, and other North County cities each have their own timelines and requirements. Projects that involve structural changes, gas line relocation, or electrical panel upgrades generally require more extensive review. We always recommend submitting permits as early in the planning process as possible.
